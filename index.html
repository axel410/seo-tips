SEO for Photographers

Your Guide to Getting Found on Google

1. The Power of Words: Text Content & Headings

Photographers' websites are visual, but search engines like Google read text, not images. To understand what a page is about, Google needs descriptive text.

Why Text Matters

  • Context for Google: Text explains the who, what, where, and why behind the photos.
  • Keywords: It allows photographers to include keywords their potential clients are searching for (e.g., "newborn photographer in Brooklyn").
  • Blog Posts: Blogging about recent shoots is a fantastic way to add fresh, relevant text content regularly.

Using Headings Correctly

Headings structure the page and tell Google what's most important.

  • <h1>: The main title of the page. Use only one per page. (e.g., "Toronto Wedding Photography")
  • <h2>: Subheadings for major sections. (e.g., "Our Wedding Packages", "Meet the Photographer")
  • <h3>: Sub-sections within an H2. (e.g., "Elopement Package")

Actionable Tip: Encourage photographers to write a few descriptive paragraphs on their homepage. This text should clearly state their services (e.g., wedding, portrait) and geographic location (e.g., "serving Austin, TX and surrounding areas"). The page should end with a clear call-to-action (CTA), like a button to their contact form or a visible phone number.

Key Pages for SEO Success

Focusing on specific pages can maximize traffic and conversions. Here are the most important ones and how to optimize them:

Homepage

  • Clearly state who you are, what you do, and where you do it.
  • Feature your best work prominently.
  • Include testimonials from happy clients.
  • Have a strong Call-to-Action (CTA) like "Book Your Session".

Services Pages

  • Create a separate page for each core service (e.g., Weddings, Portraits).
  • Use descriptive text with keywords (e.g., "candid wedding photography").
  • Showcase a gallery of relevant work on each page.

Packages / Pricing

  • Be clear and upfront about your offerings.
  • Detail what's included in each package.
  • Use headings to structure the information clearly.
  • Include a CTA to contact for custom quotes.

About Us

  • Tell your story and share your passion.
  • Include a professional photo of yourself.
  • Build trust and connect with potential clients on a personal level.

Blog

  • Share recent client shoots (with permission!).
  • Write about venues, locations, and vendors you love.
  • Provide helpful tips for clients (e.g., "What to Wear").
  • This is the best way to add fresh content regularly.

Public Galleries

  • Optimize gallery titles and descriptions with keywords.
  • Add a paragraph of text describing the shoot (the story, the location).
  • Ensure images have descriptive filenames before uploading.

2. Your Digital Storefront: Meta Title & Description

The meta title and description are what users see in Google search results. They are a crucial first impression and a major factor in whether someone clicks.

Anatomy of a Good Meta Tag

Meta Title: The blue clickable link. Should be ~50-60 characters. Must include primary keywords and the business name.

Meta Description: The short text snippet below the title. Should be ~150-160 characters. A compelling sales pitch to encourage clicks.

Homepage Examples

Title: Denver Maternity & Newborn Photographer | Willow Studios

Description: Capture the magic of motherhood with Willow Studios. We offer beautiful, timeless maternity and newborn photography in our Denver, CO studio.

Title: Home Page

Description: We take photos of people. Click here to see our gallery of work from the last few years. We have many happy clients.

Blog Post Examples

Title: 5 Tips for a Perfect Sunset Beach Photoshoot | Sun & Sea Photo

Description: Planning a beach photoshoot? Read our top 5 tips for preparing, what to wear, and how to get the most magical sunset photos.

Title: New Blog

Description: I went to the beach the other day to take some pictures and wanted to share some of them with you. Here are my thoughts on the session.

Contact Page Examples

Title: Contact a San Diego Wedding Photographer | Sarah Chen Photo

Description: Ready to book your wedding photography? Contact Sarah Chen today to check availability for your date and receive a custom quote.

Title: Contact

Description: Use the form on this page to send us a message with any questions that you might have. We will get back to you soon.

3. The Direct Line to Google: Search Console & Sitemaps

Google Search Console (GSC) is a free tool from Google that helps website owners monitor their site's performance in search results. Submitting a sitemap is a key part of this process.

Why Connect to GSC?

  • Performance Insights: See which search queries bring users to the site.
  • Health Monitoring: Get alerts for technical errors that could harm ranking.
  • Indexing Status: Confirm that Google can find and crawl all the important pages.

What is a Sitemap?

A sitemap is an XML file that lists all the important pages on a website. It's like giving Google a map to ensure it doesn't miss anything.

  • Our platform automatically generates this sitemap file.
  • The photographer just needs to copy the sitemap URL and submit it within their GSC account.

Actionable Tip: Guide photographers through the simple steps of verifying their site with GSC and submitting their sitemap. This is a one-time setup that provides long-term value.

4. Winning Local Searches: Google Business Profile

For photographers serving a specific area, a Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is the single most important tool for local SEO. It's what powers the map results (e.g., "photographer near me").

Key GMB Success Factors:

  • Complete Profile: Fill out every single section: services offered, service areas, hours, phone number, website link, attributes (e.g., women-led), etc.
  • Accurate Categories: Select primary and secondary categories accurately (e.g., "Photographer," "Wedding Photographer," "Portrait Studio").
  • Frequent Updates: Regularly add photos from recent shoots and use the "Posts" feature to announce special offers or share a link to a new blog post.
  • Gather Reviews: Actively ask happy clients to leave reviews. Positive reviews are a massive ranking factor. Respond to all reviews, both positive and negative.

Actionable Tip: Remind photographers that their GMB profile is as important as their website's homepage for attracting local clients. Encourage them to set a monthly reminder to add new photos and a post.
